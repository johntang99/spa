## Editorial Direction for Dr Huang

### What to lean into
- calm, confident, practical education
- plain-English explanations of TCM concepts
- realistic expectations, not miracle language
- trust-building for first-time patients
- conditions commonly searched by adults in suburban / small-city markets
- symptom-to-treatment decision content
- articles that reduce hesitation before booking

### What to avoid
- overhyped wellness fluff
- generic "ancient wisdom" copy pasted from other clinics
- obvious AI structure repetition
- exaggerated cure claims
- duplicate article themes across future doctor sites
- articles that sound like generic SEO landing pages

### Writing style rules
- vary sentence length and paragraph rhythm
- use a few concrete examples, not endless bullet dumps
- explain TCM ideas in modern everyday language
- keep a practitioner-informed tone, not mystical or salesy
- use local SEO lightly and naturally
- include useful nuance and reasonable limits of care

## SEO Strategy for Dr Huang

### Primary search intent buckets
1. **Pain relief intent**
2. **First-visit / trust intent**
3. **Stress / sleep / nervous system intent**
4. **Digestive symptom intent**
5. **Women’s health support intent**
6. **Seasonal / immune support intent**
7. **Treatment explainer intent**

### On-page SEO rules
Each article should include:
- one clear primary keyword target
- 2–4 natural secondary phrases
- one useful internal link to a related service
- one useful internal link to a related condition or booking/contact page
- a strong, non-clickbait title
- a believable excerpt / meta description feel
- FAQ section when useful
- concise disclaimer language where needed

### Local SEO guidance
Use location references selectively:
- Middletown, NY
- Hudson Valley
- Orange County, NY

Do **not** force location into every heading.
Use local references most naturally in:
- introductions
- conclusion / CTA
- first-visit or clinic decision articles
