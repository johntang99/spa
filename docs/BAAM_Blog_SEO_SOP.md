## 1. How Blog Articles Help SEO

Blog articles are NOT direct conversion pages. They serve three SEO functions:

### 1.1 Internal Link Authority

Each blog article that links to an SEO page passes "link authority" to that page. Google sees internal links as votes of importance. More blog articles linking to `/zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛` = higher ranking for that page.

```
Blog Article: "腰痛的5种中医治疗方法"
  └→ links to: /zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛     ← authority flows here
  └→ links to: /zh/法拉盛中医针灸       ← and here
```

**Rule of thumb:** Each SEO page should have 3-5 blog articles linking to it within 6 months.

### 1.2 Long-Tail Keyword Coverage

SEO pages target 1-2 high-value keywords. Blog articles capture hundreds of long-tail queries:

| SEO Page Keyword | Blog Long-Tail Queries |
|---|---|
| 法拉盛针灸治腰痛 | "针灸治腰痛有效吗", "腰痛针灸多少次", "针灸腰痛原理" |
| acupuncture back pain flushing | "does acupuncture work for back pain", "how many sessions for back pain" |

Each long-tail query that lands on a blog article → user reads → clicks internal link → lands on SEO page. This creates a traffic funnel.

### 1.3 Topical Authority

Google evaluates whether a site is an expert on a topic. A site with 20+ quality articles about acupuncture signals to Google: "This site is an authority on acupuncture." This lifts rankings for ALL pages on the site.

**Minimum threshold:** 10-15 articles per major topic to establish topical authority.

## 2. The Internal Link Rule

> **Every blog article MUST contain at least one link to a relevant SEO page.**

This is the single most important rule. Without internal links, blog articles are isolated islands — they attract traffic but don't help money pages rank.

### Link Placement Best Practices

**1. Early link (first 2 paragraphs):**
Place the most important SEO page link within the first 200 words. Example:

> 腰痛是现代人最常见的健康问题之一。在[法拉盛针灸治腰痛](https://www.acupunctureflushing.com/zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛)的诊疗实践中，我们发现...

**2. Contextual link (body):**
Link naturally within the content where it adds value:

> 除了针灸，[中药调理](https://www.acupunctureflushing.com/zh/法拉盛中药)也是改善慢性腰痛的有效方法。

**3. CTA link (end):**
End every article with a call-to-action linking to an SEO page or contact page:

> 如果您正在寻找法拉盛专业的针灸治疗，欢迎[预约咨询](https://www.acupunctureflushing.com/zh/contact)。

### Anchor Text Rules

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| `[法拉盛针灸治腰痛](/zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛)` | `[点击这里](/zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛)` |
| `[acupuncture for back pain in Flushing](/en/acupuncture-for-back-pain-flushing-ny)` | `[click here](/en/acupuncture-for-back-pain-flushing-ny)` |
| `[了解更多关于拔罐疗法](/zh/法拉盛拔罐)` | `[了解更多](/zh/法拉盛拔罐)` |

Use **keyword-rich anchor text** that matches the target page's main keyword. This tells Google what the linked page is about.

### How Many Links Per Article

| Article Length | Internal Links to SEO Pages | Links to Other Blog Articles |
|---|---|---|
| Short (500-800 words) | 1-2 | 1 |
| Medium (800-1200 words) | 2-3 | 1-2 |
| Long (1200-2000 words) | 3-4 | 2-3 |

**Don't over-link.** Every link should feel natural and useful to the reader.

## 3. Blog Article Structure & Requirements

### Required Elements

| Element | Requirement | SEO Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Title** | Include target keyword, under 60 chars | Ranks in search results |
| **Meta description** | Include keyword, 120-155 chars, include CTA | Improves CTR from search results |
| **H1** | Same as title or slight variation | Primary ranking signal |
| **Opening paragraph** | Answer the query directly + include first internal link | Featured snippet opportunity |
| **H2 subheadings** | 3-5 H2s using related keywords | Ranks for sub-queries |
| **Body content** | 800-1500 words, original, genuinely useful | Depth signal |
| **Internal links** | 1-3 links to SEO pages | Authority transfer |
| **FAQ section** | 3-4 Q&A pairs | FAQ schema markup |
| **CTA** | Link to contact/booking page | Conversion |
| **Images** | 1-3 with keyword-rich alt text | Image search traffic |

### Content Quality Rules

- **Answer the question immediately** — don't bury the answer after 500 words of intro
- **Be specific to your practice** — mention Dr. Jiang, Flushing, your specific approach
- **Include local signals** — mention Flushing, Queens, NY, specific neighborhoods
- **No AI-generated filler** — Google detects and devalues thin content
- **Each article must be meaningfully different** — no rewriting the same article 5 ways

## 4. Chinese vs English Blog Strategy

### Different Search Behavior = Different Articles

Chinese and English users search differently. Do NOT simply translate English articles to Chinese.

| English User Searches | Chinese User Searches |
|---|---|
| "does acupuncture work for back pain" | "针灸治腰痛有效吗" |
| "acupuncture vs physical therapy" | "针灸和理疗哪个好" |
| "what to expect first acupuncture visit" | "第一次看中医要注意什么" |
| "acupuncture side effects" | "针灸有副作用吗" |
| "how much does acupuncture cost in NY" | "纽约针灸多少钱" |

### Language-Specific Rules

**English articles:**
- Target question-based queries ("does acupuncture work for...")
- Include location: "Flushing, NY", "Queens", "New York"
- Link to English SEO pages
- Cite studies and medical sources (Western readers expect evidence)
- Tone: professional, evidence-based

**Chinese articles:**
- Target Chinese search patterns ("针灸治XX有效吗", "中医调理XX")
- Include Chinese location: "法拉盛", "纽约"
- Link to Chinese SEO pages
- Reference TCM theory (Chinese readers expect traditional knowledge)
- Tone: authoritative but approachable, like a trusted doctor
- Use simplified Chinese (not traditional) for mainland/US Chinese audience
- OK to mix some English terms where natural ("HSA", "insurance")

### Shared Topics, Different Angles

You CAN write about the same condition in both languages, but angle it differently:

| Topic | English Angle | Chinese Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Back pain | "5 Signs You Should Try Acupuncture for Back Pain" | "腰痛的中医辨证：肾虚型、血瘀型、风寒型" |
| Insomnia | "Acupuncture vs Sleeping Pills: What Research Says" | "失眠中医调理：从心肝脾肾四个角度分析" |
| Cost | "Is Acupuncture Worth the Cost? A Breakdown" | "法拉盛针灸费用与保险报销指南" |

## 5. Content Calendar & Publishing Frequency

### Recommended Frequency

| Site Stage | Frequency | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch (Month 1-3)** | 2 articles/week (1 EN + 1 ZH) | Build initial topical authority fast |
| **Growth (Month 4-6)** | 1 article/week (alternate EN/ZH) | Maintain momentum |
| **Maintenance (Month 7+)** | 2 articles/month (1 EN + 1 ZH) | Sustain freshness signal |

### Why This Frequency?

- **Too few** (1/month): Takes 2+ years to build topical authority
- **Too many** (daily): Quality drops, content becomes thin, Google devalues
- **Sweet spot** (1-2/week at launch, 2/month ongoing): Builds authority in 3-6 months without sacrificing quality

## 6. Google Search Console — Blog Indexing Strategy

### Should You Manually Index Blog Articles in GSC?

**Short answer: Only for the first 5-10 articles. After that, let Google discover them naturally.**

### Why?

| Method | Speed | Effort | When to Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Manual URL Inspection** | Fast (1-3 days) | High (must do each URL) | First 5-10 articles to jumpstart crawling |
| **Sitemap auto-discovery** | Medium (3-7 days) | None | After Google is actively crawling your site |
| **Internal link discovery** | Slow (1-2 weeks) | None | Default — Google follows links from existing pages |

### The Strategy

**Phase 1 — First 5-10 articles (Manual):**
1. Publish article
2. Go to GSC → URL Inspection → paste article URL
3. Click "Request Indexing"
4. This teaches Google: "This site publishes new content regularly, crawl it more often"

**Phase 2 — After 10 articles (Automatic):**
- Google should now be crawling your site every 1-3 days
- New articles appear in the sitemap automatically
- Google discovers them without manual submission
- Only use manual indexing if an article isn't indexed after 2 weeks

### How to Check if Auto-Crawling is Working

1. GSC → Settings → Crawl Stats
2. Look at "Total crawl requests" — should be increasing
3. Check "Average response time" — should be under 500ms
4. If Google crawls 50+ pages/day, you don't need manual indexing

### Blog-Specific GSC Monitoring

**Monthly check:**
1. GSC → Performance → filter Pages containing `/blog/`
2. Review:
   - Which articles get the most impressions?
   - Which articles get clicks?
   - Which have high impressions but low CTR? (optimize title)
   - Which queries trigger blog articles? (discover new article ideas)

**Quarterly check:**
1. GSC → Pages → filter by `/blog/`
2. Check for:
   - Articles "Crawled but not indexed" (improve content quality)
   - Articles "Duplicate without canonical" (check for similar content)
   - Soft 404s (broken article URLs)

## 8. Measuring Blog SEO Impact

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Where to Find | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|
| **Blog traffic** | GSC Performance → filter `/blog/` | Are articles attracting visitors? |
| **SEO page rankings** | GSC Performance → filter SEO page URLs | Are rankings improving as blog articles link to them? |
| **Internal link clicks** | Google Analytics → Events (if tracked) | Are readers clicking through to SEO pages? |
| **Topical authority** | GSC → # of keywords ranking on page 1 | Is the site ranking for more related terms? |
| **Crawl frequency** | GSC → Settings → Crawl Stats | Is Google crawling the site more often? |

### Monthly Review Template

- Articles to write next month: ___

## 9. Checklist — Before Publishing Any Blog Article

### Content Quality
- [ ] Answers the target query directly in the first paragraph
- [ ] 800-1500 words of original, useful content
- [ ] Not a rewrite of an existing article on the site
- [ ] Includes specific local references (Flushing, Dr. Jiang, clinic details)
- [ ] Language matches locale (simplified Chinese for zh, English for en)

### SEO Signals
- [ ] Title includes target keyword, under 60 characters
- [ ] Meta description includes keyword, 120-155 characters
- [ ] H1 matches or closely mirrors title
- [ ] 3-5 H2 subheadings with related keywords
- [ ] 1-3 images with keyword-rich alt text

### Internal Links (MOST IMPORTANT)
- [ ] At least 1 link to a relevant SEO page (Chinese article → Chinese SEO page, English → English)
- [ ] Anchor text uses target keyword (not "click here")
- [ ] First link appears within first 200 words
- [ ] CTA at end links to contact/booking page
- [ ] 1-2 links to other related blog articles (if they exist)

### FAQ Section
- [ ] 3-4 Q&A pairs included
- [ ] Questions are actual queries people search for
- [ ] Answers are concise (2-3 sentences each)

### Publishing
- [ ] Article appears in sitemap
- [ ] For first 10 articles: manually request indexing in GSC
- [ ] After 10 articles: verify auto-crawling is working, skip manual indexing
