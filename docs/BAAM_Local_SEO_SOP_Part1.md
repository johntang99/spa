## 1. Executive Summary

The SEO process is an operating system, not a one-time task. For local businesses, the complete loop is:

**Business Truth → Seed Keywords → Expanded Research → Competitor Audit → Intent Clustering → Page Mapping → On-Page SEO → Content → GBP → Technical → Citations → Reviews → Search Console → Monthly Refinement**

This SOP gives the BAAM team a repeatable, step-by-step execution guide for every stage of that loop. It is designed to be industry-agnostic — the same logic applies to acupuncture clinics, restaurants, real estate agents, dental offices, and any other local business vertical.

### Core Principles

- **One strong page per keyword cluster** — not one page per keyword
- **A ranking page must also be a conversion page** — trust, local proof, and a clear next step on every money page
- **GBP is part of the keyword system** — not a separate channel
- **Search Console is the feedback loop** — let real query data drive every next wave of pages
- **SEO begins in Stage A** — the page architecture IS the SEO architecture

## 3. Step 1 — Business Truth Collection

Before touching any keyword tool, document the ground truth of the business. This prevents keyword research from leading the strategy in the wrong direction.

### 1.1 Business Truth Intake Form

Complete this form for every new client before any keyword work begins.

**Business Identity**
- Legal business name and DBA (doing business as) name
- Primary service address (street, city, state, zip)
- Additional service locations or service-area cities
- Phone number, website URL, booking/contact URL
- Languages served (important for multi-language SEO)

**Services & Products**
- List every service offered (be exhaustive — clients often omit secondary services)
- Which services are the highest revenue? Which are the highest margin?
- Are there services the client wants to grow vs. maintain?
- Are there services competitors offer that this client does not?

**Differentiators & Credentials**
- Years in business
- Professional certifications, licenses, awards
- Practitioner names and credentials (for medical/legal verticals)
- What do existing clients most commonly say in reviews?
- What makes this business different from the 3 nearest competitors?

**Target Customer**
- Who is the primary customer? (age, problem, situation)
- What is the customer's main pain point before they search?
- What question are they most likely typing into Google?
- What objections do they typically have before booking?

**Geographic Reality**
- What is the primary city served?
- What nearby cities/towns do clients realistically come from?
- Is there a service radius? (e.g., "we serve clients within 20 miles")
- Are there any cities where the business is NOT willing to accept clients?

> **Rule:** Only claim geographic coverage that is honest. Listing fake service areas damages trust with Google and with clients.

## 4. Step 2 — Keyword Research Execution

### 2.1 Build Seed Keywords First

Before using any tool, build seed keywords manually from the business truth. Combine:

- **Service + Location**: `acupuncture middletown ny`, `chinese herbal medicine middletown`
- **Problem + Location**: `back pain treatment middletown ny`, `insomnia help middletown`
- **Trust/Comparison**: `best acupuncturist middletown ny`, `acupuncture cost middletown ny`
- **Nearby area** (only if honest): `acupuncture goshen ny`, `acupuncture orange county ny`

Aim for 15–30 seed phrases before opening any tool.

### 2.2 Free Tool Workflow

**Google Search (Primary — always use this first)**

1. Open an incognito browser window (removes personalization)
2. Type each seed keyword and note:
   - Autocomplete suggestions (these are real searches)
   - "People Also Ask" boxes (these are real questions — each is a potential FAQ or resource page)
   - "Related Searches" at the bottom (these reveal adjacent intent)
   - What page types appear on page 1 (are they service pages, directories, blog posts?)
3. Screenshot all of the above for your keyword research file

**Google Keyword Planner (Free with Google Ads account)**

1. Go to Tools → Keyword Planner → Discover New Keywords
2. Enter your 15–30 seed phrases
3. Set location to the primary city or state
4. Export the results
5. Filter for keywords with average monthly searches > 10 (local searches are small — 10–100/mo is valid)
6. Flag any keyword with low competition that the client does not currently rank for

**Google Trends**

1. Compare 2–3 variations of the same keyword (e.g., "acupuncture" vs. "acupuncturist")
2. Check seasonality — does search volume spike at certain times of year?
3. Check regional interest — is search volume concentrated in the client's area?

**Answer the Public (answerthepublic.com — free tier)**

1. Enter the primary service keyword (e.g., "acupuncture")
2. Set country to US
3. Export the question, preposition, and comparison wheels
4. Pull the most relevant questions into your keyword map as FAQ or resource page candidates

**Ubersuggest (Free tier — Neil Patel)**

1. Enter seed keywords one at a time
2. Pull keyword ideas and sort by SEO Difficulty (SD)
3. Target keywords with SD below 40 for a new or low-authority local site
4. Export and merge with your Google Keyword Planner list

### 2.3 Paid Tool Workflow (If Available)

**Ahrefs or SEMrush**

1. Enter the client's domain → Site Explorer → Organic Keywords (see what they already rank for)
2. Enter top 3 competitor domains → find keywords competitors rank for that the client does not (this is the gap map)
3. Use Keyword Explorer to get accurate volume and difficulty scores
4. Filter: volume ≥ 10/mo (local), KD ≤ 35, keyword contains city name or service term

> **BAAM Note:** If no paid tool is available, the free workflow above covers 80% of what is needed for local SEO. Do not delay keyword research waiting for a paid tool subscription.

### 2.4 Keyword Research Output Format

Store all keywords in a master spreadsheet or table with these columns:

| Keyword Phrase | Monthly Volume | Difficulty | Intent | Bucket | Target URL | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| acupuncture middletown ny | 90 | Low | Transactional | Core service | /en/acupuncture-middletown-ny | Build |
| acupuncture for back pain middletown ny | 30 | Low | Condition | Problem/condition | /en/acupuncture-for-back-pain | Build |

### 2.5 Volume Judgment for Local Businesses

Local search volumes are small. Do not discard low-volume keywords.

| Monthly Volume | Action |
|---|---|
| 500+ | High priority — build immediately |
| 100–499 | Standard priority — include in phase 1 |
| 30–99 | Build when condition/condition pages are being created |
| 10–29 | Include as FAQ or supporting content on a parent page |
| < 10 | Monitor — may be worth a paragraph, not a full page |

## 5. Step 3 — Competitor SEO Audit

### 3.1 Identify the Right Competitors

For local SEO, competitors are businesses that rank on page 1 of Google for the client's primary keywords — not necessarily the businesses the client thinks of as competitors.

**How to find SEO competitors:**

1. Search the client's 3 primary keywords in incognito
2. Note every business that appears in the Maps Pack (top 3 map results)
3. Note every business that appears in organic results positions 1–10
4. Exclude directories (Yelp, Healthgrades, ZocDoc) — these are citation opportunities, not competitors
5. You should end up with 3–6 true local competitors

### 3.2 Competitor Audit Template

For each competitor, document:

| Field | What to Record |
|---|---|
| Business name & URL | — |
| Maps Pack position | 1st, 2nd, 3rd, or not in pack |
| Organic position | For which keywords and what position |
| Number of Google reviews | And average star rating |
| GBP completeness | Photos, posts, Q&A active? |
| Site page count | Rough estimate |
| Service pages | Do they have dedicated pages per service? |
| Condition/problem pages | Do they have "acupuncture for back pain" type pages? |
| Blog/resource content | How many posts, how recent? |
| Schema markup | Use Google Rich Results Test |
| Page speed | Use PageSpeed Insights |
| NAP consistency | Does address match across web? |

### 3.3 Gap Analysis

After auditing all competitors, build a gap map:

**Content gaps** — keywords competitors rank for that the client has no page for
**GBP gaps** — competitors have more reviews, more photos, more posts
**Technical gaps** — competitors have faster pages, better schema
**Link gaps** — competitors have citations or backlinks the client lacks

This gap map becomes the priority list for the keyword-to-page mapping step.

## 6. Step 4 — Intent Clustering & Keyword Taxonomy

Every local business keyword universe fits into five buckets. Group all keywords from Step 2 into these buckets before mapping to pages.

### 6.1 The Five Keyword Buckets

| Bucket | Search Intent | Conversion Temperature | Examples |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|---------|
| **Core service** | Direct booking intent — user knows what they want | 🔥 Hottest | `acupuncture middletown ny`, `acupuncturist near me` |
| **Problem/condition** | Pain-point search — user has a problem, not a solution yet | 🔥 Hot | `back pain treatment middletown`, `help for insomnia middletown ny` |
| **Decision/comparison** | Research phase — user is comparing options before booking | 🌡️ Warm | `is acupuncture safe`, `acupuncture cost middletown ny`, `how many sessions` |
| **Brand/trust** | Navigational — user already knows the business | 🌡️ Warm | `dr huang clinic`, `dr huang clinic reviews`, `dr huang middletown` |
| **Near-location** | Geographic expansion — nearby cities client honestly serves | 🔥 Hot | `acupuncture goshen ny`, `acupuncture newburgh ny` |

### 6.2 Intent Labelling Rules

- Label each keyword with ONE primary intent — do not double-label
- When in doubt, ask: "What would I do immediately after this search?" — if the answer is "book an appointment," it is transactional
- Problem/condition keywords often do not include a city name — that is okay, add city context in the page content

### 6.3 Cluster Formation Rules

- Group keywords that could be served by the same page into one cluster
- A cluster becomes its own page only when the intent is **meaningfully different** from adjacent clusters
- Example: `acupuncture for back pain` and `acupuncture for neck pain` may share a "pain management" page, or have separate pages if volume justifies it

## 7. Step 5 — Keyword-to-Page Mapping

### 7.1 Page Type Definitions

| Page Type | DB `page_type` | Purpose | Examples | Required? |
|-----------|---------------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Homepage** | — (seo in page JSON) | Brand + primary service + location signal | Dr. Huang Clinic — Acupuncture & TCM in Middletown, NY | Yes |
| **Core landing page** | `seo-local-landing` | Primary service + city — the most important SEO page | Acupuncture in Middletown, NY | Yes |
| **Service landing pages** | `seo-service` | One service/modality with dedicated SEO page — one per service offered | Chinese Herbal Medicine in Middletown, NY; Cupping Therapy in Middletown, NY | **Yes — one per service (V3.9)** |
| **Condition page** | `seo-condition` | One condition or problem the service treats | Acupuncture for Back Pain, Acupuncture for Insomnia | Yes |
| **Resource/decision page** | `seo-resource` | Educational content for pre-booking questions | Acupuncture Cost, Your First Visit | Yes |
| **Near-location page** | `seo-near-location` | Same service, different nearby city | Acupuncture in Goshen, NY | Phase 2 |
| **FAQ page** | — | Aggregated common questions | FAQ — Dr. Huang Clinic | Phase 2 |

> **⚡ V3.9 — Service landing pages:** Every service/modality the business offers must have its own SEO landing page. The primary service (e.g., Acupuncture) maps to the `seo-local-landing` core page. All secondary services (e.g., Cupping, Herbal Medicine) each get a `seo-service` page. All SEO pages are registered in the `site_seo_pages` DB table and served by the dynamic `[slug]` route. The homepage and services page auto-link to SEO pages via `getServiceSEOLinks()` — sites without entries fall back to anchor links (no broken links).

### 7.2 Master Keyword Map Table

Maintain one master table per client site. This is the single source of truth for all SEO work.

| Keyword Cluster | Representative Phrase | Target URL | Page Type | Title Tag | H1 | Meta Description | Priority | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core local service | acupuncture middletown ny | /en/acupuncture-middletown-ny | Core landing | Acupuncture in Middletown, NY \| Dr. Huang Clinic | Acupuncture in Middletown, NY | Expert acupuncture, herbal medicine & TCM care in Middletown, NY. Book your first visit with Dr. Huang. | P1 | Build |

### 7.3 URL Structure Rules

- Always lowercase, hyphen-separated
- Include the primary keyword and city in the URL
- Keep URLs as short as possible while remaining descriptive
- For multi-language BAAM sites: `/en/[slug]`, `/zh/[slug]`
- Never change a URL after it has been indexed without setting up a 301 redirect

### 7.4 Internal Link Architecture

```
Homepage
  └── Core Landing Page (primary service + city)
        ├── Service Pages (one per major service)
        │     └── Condition Pages (one per condition per service)
        ├── Near-Location Pages (one per honest service city)
        └── Resource Pages (decision/comparison content)
              └── FAQ Page
```

**Internal linking rules:**
- Every page links up to its parent
- Every parent page links down to its children
- Core landing page links to all service and condition pages
- All condition pages link to the core landing page with anchor text containing the primary keyword
- Every money page has a CTA that links to the contact/booking page

## 8. Step 6 — On-Page SEO Implementation

### 8.1 Element-by-Element Rules

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **URL** | Short, keyword-rich, city-inclusive | `/en/acupuncture-middletown-ny` |
| **Title tag** | Primary keyword + city + brand name, under 60 characters | `Acupuncture in Middletown, NY \| Dr. Huang Clinic` |
| **H1** | Close match to title, human-readable, only one per page | `Acupuncture in Middletown, NY` |
| **Meta description** | Under 155 characters, include keyword + city + CTA | `Expert acupuncture & TCM in Middletown, NY. Back pain, insomnia, stress relief. Book your first visit with Dr. Huang today.` |
| **H2 headings** | Each major section gets an H2 — include secondary keywords naturally | `What Conditions Does Acupuncture Treat?` |
| **Body copy** | Include primary keyword in first 100 words, naturally throughout | — |
| **Images** | Alt text describes the image with keyword context | `Dr. Huang performing acupuncture treatment in Middletown NY clinic` |
| **NAP block** | Full name, address, phone — must match GBP exactly | In footer of every page |
| **CTA** | Every money page has a visible, above-the-fold CTA | `Book Your First Appointment` |

### 8.2 Schema Markup Required Per Page Type

| Page Type | Schema Types Required |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Homepage | `LocalBusiness`, `MedicalClinic` (or industry equivalent) |
| Core landing page (`seo-local-landing`) | `LocalBusiness`, `Service`, `BreadcrumbList` |
| Service landing page (`seo-service`) | `Service`, `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList` |
| Condition page (`seo-condition`) | `Service`, `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList` |
| Resource page (`seo-resource`) | `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList` |
| Near-location page (`seo-near-location`) | `LocalBusiness`, `Service`, `BreadcrumbList` |
| All pages | `WebPage`, `Organization` |

Validate all schema at: **search.google.com/test/rich-results**

### 8.3 Page Body Structure Template

Every money page (core landing, service, condition) should follow this structure:

1. **Hero section** — H1, primary keyword, city, strong value proposition, CTA button
2. **Trust bar** — years in practice, credentials, number of patients, reviews badge
3. **What we treat / What this service does** — clear explanation, conditions addressed
4. **Our approach** — what makes this practitioner/clinic different
5. **Testimonials** — 3 reviews mentioning the specific service and city
6. **FAQ section** — 4–6 questions from "People Also Ask" research
7. **Location/contact section** — map embed, address, phone, hours
8. **Final CTA** — repeat the booking CTA

## 9. Step 7 — Content Writing Guidelines

### 9.1 Content Philosophy

Every ranking page must also be a conversion page. This means:

- **People-first**: Write for the patient/customer, not for Google
- **Local-specific**: Mention the city, neighborhood, or region naturally — not mechanically
- **Trust-building**: Include credentials, reviews, photos, and specific experience
- **Action-oriented**: Every section moves the reader toward a booking decision

### 9.2 Word Count Guidelines Per Page Type

| Page Type | Minimum Words | Target Words |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Homepage | 600 | 800–1,200 |
| Core landing page (`seo-local-landing`) | 800 | 1,000–1,500 |
| Service landing page (`seo-service`) | 400 | 600–900 |
| Condition page (`seo-condition`) | 500 | 700–1,000 |
| Resource/decision page (`seo-resource`) | 700 | 1,000–1,500 |
| Near-location page (`seo-near-location`) | 400 | 600–800 |
| FAQ page | 400 | 600–1,000 |

> **Quality over length:** A 600-word page that answers the searcher's question perfectly outranks a 2,000-word page that is thin and repetitive.

### 9.3 Keyword Usage Rules

- **Primary keyword**: Include in H1, first 100 words of body, at least 2–3 more times naturally, and in the meta description
- **Secondary keywords**: Include naturally in H2 headings and body paragraphs — do not force
- **City/location**: Mention 3–5 times naturally — once in H1, once in the first paragraph, in the NAP block, and in 1–2 body paragraphs
- **Keyword density**: No formal target — write naturally, then check that it does not feel robotic
- **Forbidden**: Keyword stuffing, hidden text, exact-match keyword repetition in consecutive sentences

### 9.4 FAQ Writing Standard

Each FAQ answer should:
- Directly answer the question in the first sentence
- Be 50–150 words
- Include a natural reference to the service, clinic, and location
- End with a soft CTA when appropriate ("Book a consultation to discuss your situation")

Source FAQ questions from:
- Google "People Also Ask" boxes
- GBP Q&A section (real questions from real patients)
- Front desk staff's most common new-patient questions
- Review content (what do people ask before they book?)

### 9.5 AI-Assisted Content Workflow

For BAAM's Pipeline B (automated onboarding), content is AI-generated and then reviewed:

1. **AI draft**: Generate page copy using the keyword map, business truth, and page structure template
2. **Human review**: Check for accuracy (credentials, services, hours), naturalness, and local specificity
3. **Client approval**: For medical and legal verticals, client must approve before publishing
4. **Publish**: Deploy and submit URL for indexing in Search Console

> **Rule**: AI-generated content for medical verticals must always be reviewed by a human before publishing. Inaccurate medical information damages trust and can harm patients.

## 10. Step 8 — Google Business Profile Integration

### 10.1 GBP as a Keyword Channel

GBP should be managed as part of the keyword system. Each major keyword cluster should have a corresponding GBP support layer.

| GBP Element | How to Optimize for SEO |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **Business name** | Use the exact legal name — do not stuff keywords |
| **Primary category** | Choose the most specific, accurate category available |
| **Secondary categories** | Add up to 9 additional categories matching real services |
| **Business description** | 750 characters — include primary keywords, services, and city naturally |
| **Services list** | Add every service with a description containing relevant keywords |
| **Posts** | Weekly posts targeting priority keyword clusters (pain, sleep, fertility, stress) |
| **Q&A** | Seed with 5–10 real patient questions and detailed answers |
| **Photos** | 20+ photos: exterior, interior, practitioner, treatment rooms, staff |
| **Reviews** | Actively collect — see Section 12 |

### 10.2 GBP-to-Keyword Cluster Mapping

| Keyword Cluster | GBP Support Action |
|---|---|
| Core service (acupuncture middletown) | Primary category = Acupuncturist; description mentions Middletown |
| Pain conditions | Post: "Treating back pain with acupuncture"; Service: Pain Management |
| Sleep conditions | Post: "Acupuncture for better sleep"; Q&A: "Does acupuncture help insomnia?" |
| Fertility | Service: "Fertility Acupuncture"; Post: "Supporting fertility naturally" |
| Decision/trust | Q&A: "How much does acupuncture cost?", "What should I expect on my first visit?" |

### 10.3 GBP Posting Calendar

Post at minimum once per week. Rotate through these post types:

| Week | Post Type | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Week 1 | Service highlight | "What is cupping therapy and who is it for?" |
| Week 2 | Condition education | "5 signs acupuncture could help your back pain" |
| Week 3 | Trust/social proof | Share a patient success story or recent review |
| Week 4 | Offer or event | New patient special, seasonal health tip, clinic update |

## 11. Technical SEO Audit Checklist

Run this checklist for every new BAAM site before launch and repeat quarterly.

### 11.1 Core Web Vitals & Speed

- [ ] Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) < 2.5 seconds on mobile
- [ ] Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) < 0.1
- [ ] Interaction to Next Paint (INP) < 200ms
- [ ] All hero images use Next.js `<Image>` component with correct `priority` prop
- [ ] All images have explicit `width` and `height` attributes
- [ ] WebP format used for all images where possible
- [ ] No render-blocking scripts in `<head>`
- [ ] PageSpeed Insights score ≥ 85 on mobile

**Tool:** pagespeed.web.dev

### 11.2 Crawlability & Indexing

- [ ] `robots.txt` exists and does not block important pages
- [ ] XML sitemap exists at `/sitemap.xml`
- [ ] Sitemap is submitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] All key pages return HTTP 200 status
- [ ] No important pages are set to `noindex`
- [ ] Canonical tags are correct on all pages
- [ ] No duplicate content issues (www vs non-www, http vs https)
- [ ] HTTPS is active with valid SSL certificate

**Tool:** Google Search Console → Coverage report

### 11.3 Mobile Optimization

- [ ] Site passes Google Mobile-Friendly Test
- [ ] Tap targets (buttons, links) are at least 44x44px
- [ ] Text is readable without zooming (minimum 16px body font)
- [ ] No horizontal scroll on mobile
- [ ] Phone number is a clickable `tel:` link on mobile

**Tool:** search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly

### 11.4 Structured Data / Schema

- [ ] `LocalBusiness` schema on homepage with correct NAP
- [ ] `Service` schema on all service pages
- [ ] `FAQPage` schema on FAQ and resource pages
- [ ] `BreadcrumbList` schema on all interior pages
- [ ] All schema validates without errors in Rich Results Test
- [ ] No schema for content not present on the page

**Tool:** search.google.com/test/rich-results

### 11.5 On-Page Technical Elements

- [ ] Every page has a unique title tag (under 60 characters)
- [ ] Every page has a unique meta description (under 155 characters)
- [ ] Every page has exactly one H1
- [ ] H2–H6 structure is logical and hierarchical
- [ ] All images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] No broken internal links
- [ ] No broken external links
- [ ] Footer NAP matches GBP exactly

**Tool:** Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs) or Ahrefs Site Audit

### 11.6 BAAM Platform-Specific Checks

- [ ] ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration) is configured correctly
- [ ] Multi-language routes are set up with correct `hreflang` tags
- [ ] Admin content changes trigger correct page revalidation
- [ ] JSON content files for all SEO pages are populated
- [ ] No console errors on any key page
- [ ] All environment variables are set in production

## 12. Review Collection System

### 12.1 Why Reviews Are Critical

Google's local ranking algorithm weighs review quantity, recency, and quality heavily. A business with 50 reviews at 4.8 stars will almost always outrank a competitor with 10 reviews at 4.9 stars.

**Minimum review targets:**
- Launch: 10+ reviews before considering the GBP profile competitive
- Month 3: 25+ reviews
- Month 6: 50+ reviews
- Ongoing: 2–4 new reviews per month minimum

### 12.2 Review Request System

**Primary channel: SMS (highest conversion rate)**

Send within 24 hours of appointment:

> "Hi [Name], thank you for visiting Dr. Huang Clinic today. If you have a moment, we'd love to hear about your experience. Your review helps other patients find us: [Google Review Link]. Thank you!"

**Secondary channel: Email**

Subject: "How was your visit with Dr. Huang?"

Body: Brief thank-you, single CTA button to Google review link.

**In-person channel: QR code card**

Place a small card at checkout:
> "Enjoyed your visit? Leave us a review!" with QR code linking directly to Google review page.

### 12.3 Google Review Link Generation

1. Go to your Google Business Profile
2. Click "Share profile"
3. Copy the review link
4. Use a URL shortener (bit.ly) for SMS use

### 12.4 Review Response Protocol

**Respond to every review — positive and negative.**

**Positive review response template:**
> "Thank you, [Name]! We're so glad the [service] helped with your [condition]. It's a pleasure having you as a patient at our Middletown clinic. See you at your next visit!"

**Negative review response template:**
> "Thank you for sharing your experience, [Name]. We're sorry your visit didn't meet your expectations. Please contact us directly at [phone] so we can make this right. We value every patient at Dr. Huang Clinic."

**Rules:**
- Respond within 48 hours
- Never argue or be defensive in a public response
- Never mention specific medical information in a response (HIPAA)
- Use the business name and city naturally in positive responses (this is a minor ranking signal)

### 12.5 Review Platform Priority

| Platform | Priority | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| Google Business Profile | P1 | Direct ranking signal for local search |
| Yelp | P2 | High domain authority, often ranks on page 1 |
| Healthgrades (medical) | P2 | Ranks well for medical searches |
| ZocDoc (medical) | P3 | Booking platform with SEO value |
| Facebook | P3 | Social proof for paid and organic |

## 13. Citation Building Playbook

### 13.1 What Is a Citation?

A citation is any online mention of a business's Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP). Consistent citations across the web are a ranking signal for local search.

**NAP consistency rule:** The business name, address, and phone number must be **character-for-character identical** across every listing. "St." vs. "Street" or "(845) 000-0000" vs. "845-000-0000" are inconsistencies that dilute local ranking power.

### 13.2 Citation Building Order

Build citations in this order — highest authority first:

**Tier 1 — Universal (every business)**
1. Google Business Profile
2. Bing Places for Business
3. Apple Maps (via Apple Maps Connect)
4. Facebook Business Page
5. Yelp

**Tier 2 — Industry-Specific (medical/TCM)**
6. Healthgrades
7. Zocdoc
8. WebMD Health
9. Vitals.com
10. RateMDs

**Tier 3 — Local Authority**
11. Local Chamber of Commerce directory
12. City/county business directory
13. Local newspaper business listings
14. Community organization websites

**Tier 4 — General Directories**
15. Yellow Pages (yellowpages.com)
16. Superpages
17. Foursquare
18. Better Business Bureau (BBB)
19. Mapquest
20. Hotfrog

### 13.3 Citation Audit Process

Before building new citations, audit existing ones:

1. Search `"[Business Name]" "[City]" "[State]"` in Google
2. Check every result for NAP accuracy
3. Log all inconsistencies in the citation tracking sheet

## 14. Search Console Monitoring Playbook

### 14.1 Initial Setup (One-Time)

- [ ] Verify domain property in Google Search Console (DNS verification preferred)
- [ ] Submit sitemap.xml
- [ ] Link Search Console to Google Analytics (if applicable)
- [ ] Inspect homepage URL — confirm it is indexed
- [ ] Inspect all key landing pages — confirm they are indexed

### 14.2 Monthly Monitoring — 4 Core Reports

**Report 1: Performance → Queries**
- Filter: Last 28 days vs. previous 28 days
- Look for: Queries with high impressions but low CTR (< 3%) — these need better title tags or meta descriptions
- Look for: New queries appearing that the site was not targeting — these reveal new page opportunities
- Action: If a page gets 100+ impressions/month for a keyword it was not built for, consider creating a dedicated page

**Report 2: Performance → Pages**
- Look for: Pages with declining clicks or impressions — may need content refresh
- Look for: Pages with high position (1–5) but low CTR — improve title/meta
- Look for: Pages that have never appeared — check indexing status

**Report 3: Coverage → Errors**
- Fix all "Not found (404)" errors immediately
- Fix all "Server error (5xx)" errors immediately
- Investigate "Excluded" pages — are any important pages excluded that should be indexed?

**Report 4: Core Web Vitals**
- Check for pages flagged as "Poor" — fix before next monthly review
- Confirm no regression after site updates

### 14.3 Decision Rules for Search Console Data

| Observation | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Query gets 200+ impressions, position 11–20, CTR < 2% | Strengthen that page's title tag, H1, and internal links |
| Page ranks for keyword it was not built for | Add that keyword to the page's cluster; consider a new supporting page |
| Page impressions drop > 20% month-over-month | Check for algorithm update; audit for content quality issues |
| Two pages ranking for same query | Consolidate — 301 redirect weaker page to stronger, or merge content |
| New page gets no impressions after 4 weeks | Inspect in Search Console; check indexing; add internal links from existing pages |

### 14.4 Quarterly Keyword Map Review

Every 3 months:
1. Pull all queries from Search Console for the last 90 days
2. Compare against the keyword map — are there queries not in the map?
3. Add new keyword clusters to the map for the next quarter's content build
4. Retire or consolidate any clusters where pages are not gaining traction after 6+ months

## 18. Multilingual SEO — Chinese Language Pages

> **Added:** April 2026
> **Context:** Proven workflow from acu-flushing Chinese SEO implementation.

### Why Multilingual SEO Requires Separate Architecture

Chinese SEO is **not translation**. Chinese users search differently from English users — different keywords, different intent patterns, different trust signals. A translated English page will not rank for Chinese queries.

**Core principle:** Build Chinese pages around **Chinese search behavior**, not by translating English page slugs.

### URL Strategy for Chinese Pages

**Use Chinese characters in URLs (recommended):**
```
/zh/法拉盛中医针灸/
/zh/法拉盛针灸治腰痛/
/zh/法拉盛失眠针灸/
```

**Why Chinese characters beat pinyin:**
- Google indexes and ranks Chinese-character URLs for Chinese queries
- URLs match the exact search phrase — maximum keyword relevance
- Chinese users recognize and trust Chinese-character URLs
- Pinyin is ambiguous (multiple characters share the same pinyin)

**Technical requirements:**
- Next.js auto-decodes URL params, but verify with `decodeURIComponent()` in route handlers
- `permanentRedirect()` requires URI-encoded destinations: `encodeURIComponent()` each path segment
- Sitemaps must use percent-encoded URLs (standard XML escaping)
- Filesystem supports UTF-8 filenames natively

### Page Architecture — Chinese vs. English

Do NOT mirror English pages 1:1. Build Chinese pages by **Chinese search intent**:

| Intent Type | English Example | Chinese Example |
|---|---|---|
| Core local landing | `acupuncture-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛中医针灸` |
| Condition — back pain | `acupuncture-for-back-pain-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛针灸治腰痛` |
| Condition — insomnia | `acupuncture-for-insomnia-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛失眠针灸` |
| Service — herbal medicine | `chinese-herbal-medicine-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛中药` |
| Service — cupping | `cupping-therapy-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛拔罐` |
| Cost / pricing | `acupuncture-cost-flushing-ny` | `法拉盛针灸费用` |

**Chinese-specific pages to consider (no English equivalent):**
- `法拉盛针灸师` — practitioner intent
- `法拉盛中医诊所` — clinic intent
- `法拉盛痛症针灸` — pain-focused intent
- `法拉盛妇科中医` — gynecology intent
- `法拉盛老中医` — experienced practitioner trust signal

### Migration: Replacing English-Slug Chinese Pages

When replacing old English-slug zh pages with Chinese-character URLs:

**Step 1 — Set up 301 redirects (NEVER delete old URLs without redirects)**

Create `content/<siteId>/redirects.json`:
```json
{
  "hreflang": [
    { "en": "acupuncture-flushing-ny", "zh": "法拉盛中医针灸" }
  ],
  "redirects": [
    {
      "source": "/zh/acupuncture-flushing-ny",
      "destination": "/zh/法拉盛中医针灸",
      "permanent": true
    }
  ]
}
```

**Step 2 — Create new Chinese-character page files**

Files go in `content/<siteId>/zh/` with no extension (same as English SEO pages).
Each file must include the `service` or `condition` field so the system can resolve service links:
```json
{
  "pageType": "seo-service",
  "service": "chinese-herbal-medicine",
  "seo": {
    "canonicalUrl": "/zh/法拉盛中药",
    "title": "法拉盛中药｜专业中草药调理",
    ...
  }
}
```

**Step 3 — Import to DB and register in site_seo_pages**

1. Import via admin panel (Import Locale JSON for zh)
2. Register in `site_seo_pages` table with correct `page_type`
3. Delete old zh English-slug entries from `content_entries` table

**Step 4 — Verify**

- [ ] New Chinese URLs render correctly
- [ ] Old English URLs redirect (308) to new Chinese URLs
- [ ] Homepage/services "Learn More" links point to Chinese URLs (not redirects)
- [ ] Sitemap includes Chinese URLs under `/zh/` only
- [ ] Sitemap excludes old redirected URLs
- [ ] hreflang tags pair English ↔ Chinese pages

### SEO Metadata — All Signals Must Match Page Language

For every Chinese page, ALL of these must be in Chinese:

| Signal | Example |
|---|---|
| URL slug | `法拉盛中医针灸` |
| `<title>` | `法拉盛中医针灸｜专业针灸与中医调理` |
| `<meta description>` | `法拉盛专业中医针灸诊所，Dr. Jiang医师提供传统中医治疗...` |
| H1 | `法拉盛中医针灸｜专业针灸与中医调理` |
| Body content | Full Chinese |
| FAQ questions & answers | Full Chinese |
| Internal link anchor text | `法拉盛针灸治腰痛`, not `Acupuncture for Back Pain` |
| Schema text (where applicable) | Chinese |

**Critical:** The site-wide `seo.json` for zh locale must also be in Chinese — it controls metadata for homepage, services, about, contact, and all other non-SEO pages.

### Chinese Keyword Research Approach

Chinese users in the US search with mixed patterns:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Full Chinese | `法拉盛针灸` |
| Chinese + English location | `flushing 针灸` |
| Chinese + English service | `acupuncture 法拉盛` |
| Full Chinese with specifics | `法拉盛中医调理失眠` |

**Keyword structure:** `[location in Chinese] + [service/condition in Chinese]`
- Location: `法拉盛` (Flushing), `大颈` (Great Neck)
- Services: `针灸`, `中药`, `拔罐`, `推拿`, `艾灸`
- Conditions: `腰痛`, `失眠`, `焦虑`, `痛症`
- Modifiers: `诊所`, `医师`, `费用`, `保险`

### Technical Implementation Checklist

**Routing & Rendering:**
- [ ] `[slug]/page.tsx` decodes URL params: `decodeURIComponent(params.slug)`
- [ ] Redirect destinations are URI-encoded for HTTP Location header
- [ ] Redirects fire BEFORE DB lookup (not after 404)
- [ ] `lib/redirects.ts` caches redirect lookups per site

**Service Link Resolution:**
- [ ] `getServiceSEOLinks()` checks `content_entries` for locale-specific pages
- [ ] Service matching uses the `service` field from page JSON data
- [ ] Chinese pages resolve directly (no redirect hop)

**Sitemap:**
- [ ] Chinese-character URLs included (percent-encoded in XML)
- [ ] Old redirected URLs excluded via `checkSiteRedirect()`
- [ ] Non-ASCII slugs only appear under matching locale (`/zh/`)
- [ ] Filesystem discovery picks up non-.json SEO page files

**Database:**
- [ ] New Chinese pages registered in `site_seo_pages` table
- [ ] Old zh English-slug `content_entries` deleted
- [ ] hreflang mapping stored in `redirects.json`
